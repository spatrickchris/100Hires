LinkedIn organic content strategy for B2B SaaS in Indonesia

Research material for a playbook on how B2B SaaS companies win with organic LinkedIn
content in the Indonesian market.

Why this angle:
Indonesia has around 28 million LinkedIn users and a real SaaS scene
(Mekari, Qiscus, and a long tail of HRIS/CRM/omnichannel players), but nearly every
LinkedIn playbook out there is written for the US. The Bahasa/English split, the fact
that the actual sales conversation happens on WhatsApp, and the amount of buyer
education needed here make the local strategy genuinely different. That gap is what
this research is for.

What's in here:
research/sources.md — the 10 experts I'm tracking, grouped by role, with notes on
why each one made the cut and a bench of backups
research/linkedin-posts/ — posts collected per author, one markdown file per post
research/youtube-transcripts/ — transcripts pulled per author with the script below
research/other/ — long-form interviews, conference talks, market reports
scripts/fetch_transcripts.py — pulls YouTube transcripts (prefers Bahasa captions,
falls back to English, with Supadata as a paid-tier backup for stubborn videos)



How the experts were picked:
Three groups that cover each other's blind spots: LinkedIn-native strategists who
practice B2B social selling in Indonesia, SaaS founders whose companies grew on
founder-led content, and long-form YouTube/podcast voices with frameworks and local
market data. Full reasoning per person is in research/sources.md.

Collection notes:
YouTube transcripts come through the script. LinkedIn posts are collected by hand scraping LinkedIn breaks its terms of service and gets accounts restricted, and
copying 10–20 posts per author manually turns out to be both safer and cleaner, since
each post gets annotated as it comes in. Every file carries its source URL and the
date it was collected.
