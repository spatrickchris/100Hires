## LinkedIn Authors

**1. Andryaas Mamuaya** — Founder, TribeIndo
[https://www.linkedin.com/in/andryaasmamuaya/](https://www.linkedin.com/in/andryaasmamuaya/)
30+ years in oil & gas project management before pivoting into LinkedIn B2B social selling. Now coaches C-levels and founders on getting B2B clients through LinkedIn, and his own client acquisition runs on the exact method he teaches, which is the whole reason he's on this list. Posts in a mix of Bahasa and English. His angle — trust-building before any DM, positioning clarity — is the closest thing Indonesia has to a native LinkedIn social selling doctrine.

**2. Ricky Halim** — Brand & Communication Lead, Smart Salary (HRIS/payroll SaaS)
[https://www.linkedin.com/in/ricky-halim-a05666141/](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ricky-halim-a05666141/)
The rare in-house B2B SaaS marketer here who writes openly about what he's doing: HRIS buyer surveys, B2B branding for the Indonesian market, behavior-shift predictions. He's not selling a course, which makes his takes less biased than the coaching crowd. His feed doubles as a live example of an employee-led content program at an Indonesian SaaS company.

**3. Cindi Wirawan** — Founder, Vibe Tribe (LinkedIn Top Voice)  
[https://www.linkedin.com/in/cindiw/](https://www.linkedin.com/in/cindiw/)  
Indonesian, based in Singapore. Her niche is careers/coaching rather than SaaS, so she's here for craft, not strategy: her Bahasa-English code-switching posts are among the best-written on the Indonesian feed and show how hooks and rhythm work in this market. Useful reference when the playbook gets to actual writing.

**4. Suwandi Soh** — CEO, Mekari
[https://www.linkedin.com/in/suwandisoh/](https://www.linkedin.com/in/suwandisoh/)
Mekari is the largest B2B SaaS in Indonesia (Talenta, Jurnal, Qontak, Klikpajak — 55k+ businesses). ~13k followers, ~400 posts, plus long-form interviews (the Endgame episode with Gita Wirjawan is the deepest one) and Mekari Conference keynotes. He's the reference case for how an Indonesian SaaS CEO does market education for buyers with low digital literacy. A lot of his distribution runs through the Mekari company page, so the interesting comparison is personal voice vs. corporate page.

[https://www.linkedin.com/in/anthony-kosasih-35a32799/](https://www.linkedin.com/in/anthony-kosasih-35a32799/)
The operator's counterpart to #4 — SME accounting go-to-market from inside the same company. Having both lets me compare CEO vs. COO content angles at the one Indonesian SaaS that's clearly won.

**6. Delta Purna Widyangga** — CEO & Co-founder, Qiscus
[https://www.linkedin.com/in/deltapurna/](https://www.linkedin.com/in/deltapurna/)
Qiscus (Yogyakarta) sells an omnichannel conversation platform to 1,000+ businesses and markets almost entirely through education — webinars, case studies, community. Delta speaks regularly on conversational CX. Interesting because Qiscus proves the education-led motion works outside Jakarta and outside the HR/finance categories Mekari owns.

**7. Angga Fauzan** — CEO & Co-founder, MySkill  
[https://www.linkedin.com/in/angga-fauzan](https://www.linkedin.com/in/angga-fauzan)  
Probably the most-followed startup-CEO creator on Indonesian LinkedIn; MySkill grew to 1M+ users largely on his organic content. Edtech and partly B2C, so the ICP differs from pure B2B SaaS — but nobody demonstrates the content *engine* (hooks, cadence, community activation, event funnels) better in Bahasa. He also runs sessions on monetizing as a LinkedIn creator, so he teaches the machine, not just runs it.

**8. Iwan Setiawan** — CEO, Marketeers & MarkPlus, Inc.
YouTube: [https://www.youtube.com/@MarketeersTV](https://www.youtube.com/@MarketeersTV)  
LinkedIn: [https://www.linkedin.com/in/iwansetiawan/](https://www.linkedin.com/in/iwansetiawan/)  
 Co-author of Marketing 4.0/5.0/6.0 with Philip Kotler and the strategy backbone of this list. Marketeers TV gives long transcripts applying the 5A framework (Aware–Appeal–Ask–Act–Advocate) to Indonesian companies with local data. B2B SaaS content strategy here has to sit on top of this kind of omnichannel thinking, because the Indonesian buyer moves from LinkedIn to WhatsApp fast.

YouTube: [https://www.youtube.com/@DennySantoso](https://www.youtube.com/@DennySantoso)  
LinkedIn: [https://www.linkedin.com/in/dennysantoso/](https://www.linkedin.com/in/dennysantoso/)

Huge transcript archive.

**10. Ryan Kristo Muljono** — CEO, ToffeeDev  
YouTube: [https://www.youtube.com/@ToffeeDev](https://www.youtube.com/@ToffeeDev)  
LinkedIn: [https://www.linkedin.com/in/ryankristo/](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ryankristo/)
